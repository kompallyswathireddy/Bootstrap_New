Physics Homework

1a. Explain why steel pans have handles made of plastic or wood.
Steel pans have handles made of plastic or wood because these materials are poor conductors of heat, meaning they are thermal insulators. This property prevents heat from the pan from transferring to the handle, allowing it to remain cool and safe to touch. Metals like steel are a good conductor of heat, which would cause the handle to become dangerously hot.

1b. Suggest which material, felt or paper, is the better insulator. Give a reason for your answer.
Felt is the better insulator because it has more air in its fibers, which reduces heat transfer. Air is a bad conductor of heat, and the structure of felt has better insulation compared to the thin and compact structure of paper.

2a. Choose a material you would use to line a pair of winter boots. Give a reason for your choice of material.
Wool would be a good material to line winter boots because it is an excellent insulator, trapping air in its fibers to retain heat. Additionally, wool is breathable and can wick moisture away, keeping feet warm and dry.

2b. Describe how you could carry out a test on three different lining materials. Assume you have a thermometer and a stopwatch, and you can wrap the lining around a container of hot water.
Wrap equal amounts of the three materials (e.g. wool, felt, cotton) around identical containers filled with hot water. Use a thermometer to measure the initial temperature of the water in each container. Start a stopwatch and record the temperature of the water at regular intervals (e.g. every 5 minutes) for a set time (e.g. 30 minutes). Compare the temperature changes over time. The material that shows the smallest temperature drop is the best insulator.
